What is Diabetic Retinopathy?
Complication of diabetes that affects the eyes.
The diabetic retinopathy is due to blood vessel damage in the tissue at the back of the eye (retina). THE poor follow-up of blood sugar is one of the risk factors.
The first symptoms are floaters, a blur effect, dark areas in vision, as well as difficulty perceiving colors. It can even lead to blindness.
Mild cases can be treated by strict diabetes control, while that advanced cases may require surgery or laser treatment.
Different Stages Of Diabetic Retinopathy
Stage 1: mild non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy
Stage 2: Moderate non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy
Stage 3: severe non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy
Stage 4: proliferative diabetic retinopathy
